Zune’d: Never has so much been said by so many over so little

"Never was so much owed by so many to so few."

British Prime Minister Winston Churchill
20 August 1940
In a tribute to the Royal Air Force during WWII

Once again my apologies to Sir Winston...  

I remember perusing a published article regarding a new “modern” workflow development tool where it stated, “You can even add conditions.” I couldn’t help but let out a chuckle. Seriously?! People, the ability to add conditions to a workflow is like adding a steering wheel to a car. “You can even turn the wheel!” If that’s what’s being advertised, you can imagine what is (or rather what isn’t) under the hood.

How about the slick demos? Nice suits. Nods of approval at the designated points in the presentation. It all looks good until you take the product home. Sure, you can do exactly what you saw during the presentation. But then you try to change it. Oops! Time to break out the hammer and chisel as you roll up your sleeves to code by hand. You extrapolated from what you saw – reasonably – assuming that the product was grounded in a coherent development framework. You assumed incorrectly. Welcome to the new world.

I could not recruit competent people at this one outsourcing company to develop with Notes/Domino. They were defiant in not wanting to use “primitive” software tools. Three years later the entire operation was shut down and replaced after blowing past their development budgets and deadlines. What would you expect? People can fill the air with words about what is “modern” and what is “primitive”, but at the end of day you can’t hide results.

“I wish my horse had the speed of your tongue.”

William Shakespeare
